Marisol was developed as an 8-week student project by a team of 23 second-year students from Breda University of Applied Sciences. This project provided experience working in a large interdisciplinary team, coordinating with artists and designers, and implementing core gameplay systems in Unreal Engine 5. Dive into the world of Marisol and retrieve the magical crystal to save the local people. During your adventure, enemies will try to hold you back, and there will be puzzles that require solving.

## üí´ My Contributions
### Camera System
As part of the 3C's strike team, I worked on refining the camera to make it feel natural and responsive for the player. The system includes several sophisticated features:

### Key Implementations:

- Smooth transitions between over-the-shoulder and distant camera views
- Anti-clipping system preventing camera collision with walls and objects
- Trigger-based camera focus system that temporarily locks onto specific objects
- Dynamic player slowdown and restricted camera control during focus events
- Pitch limitation preventing excessive upward/downward camera tilt
- Adaptive camera arm length that extends when looking up and shortens when looking down

## üîß Gameplay Systems
### Shield Mechanics
I implemented the main character's shield defense system, which serves as a core combat mechanic. The shield can be activated by pressing a button and provides the following functionality:

- Blocks incoming damage from enemies
- Restricts player movement while active, creating tactical decision-making
- Protects against projectile attacks

Additionally, I integrated the ground ripple projectile attack to properly deal damage to the player, completing the enemy-player interaction loop.

### Respawn System
I created a player respawn system that handles death and revival gracefully:
System Flow:

1. Upon death, player teleports to the starting location after a 2-second delay
2. Health is reset to maximum and the health widget updates accordingly
3. Screen fades to black for smooth visual transition
4. Movement and camera controls are restricted during the respawn process to prevent disorienting player input

This ensures players can quickly return to gameplay without jarring transitions or confusing states.
