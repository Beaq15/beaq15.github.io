Shell Shocked was developed as an 8-week group project during my first year at university with a team of 14 people. This project provided hands-on experience with Unreal Engine 5, team collaboration in a large group setting, shader research, gameplay programming, and the full development pipeline from concept to polished product. ## ðŸŽ® Project Overview
Shell Shocked is a chaotic 2v2 couch co-op game where players control mechanized hermit crabs competing to push a mine into the opposing team's goal. The game combines fast-paced action with strategic gameplay as players stun opponents, shoot shells, and knock the ball around the arena to score points.

> More information on the [Itch.io page](https://robbinverwijs.itch.io/shellshocked).

**Tech Stack:** Unreal Engine 5, C++, Blueprints

**Team Size:** 14 people (8-week project)

### Key Features:

- 2v2 local multiplayer gameplay
- Physics-based ball mechanics with shooting and stunning
- Dynamic camera effects (slow motion, freeze frames)
- Haptic feedback integration for controller immersion
- Custom water shader research (UE5 water plugin)

## Gameplay Features - Camera Effects
I implemented dynamic camera effects to enhance the game's impact during key moments:

- Slow Motion System - Triggered during goal scoring moments to emphasize successful plays and create dramatic highlight moments
- Freeze Frames - Applied at endgame to punctuate the match conclusion and give players a moment to process the outcome

These systems add visual polish and improve game feel by making important events more memorable.

### Haptic Feedback Integration
Implemented haptic feedback for various player actions to increase immersion and provide tactile response:

- Stun Feedback - Controller vibration when players get stunned by opponents
- Shell Pickup - Haptic response when collecting or shooting shells
- Impact Events - Feedback for ball collisions and player interactions

This sensory feedback layer helps players understand game state without relying solely on visual cues.

### Bug Fixing & Polish
I dedicated significant development time to bug fixing and quality assurance to ensure Shell Shocked was as polished and presentable as possible for the final showcase. This involved playtesting, identifying edge cases, and coordinating with team members to resolve issues across different systems.
